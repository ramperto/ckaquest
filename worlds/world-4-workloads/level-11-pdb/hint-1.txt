Check the PDB status to see how many disruptions are currently allowed:

  kubectl get pdb pdb-app-pdb -n ckaquest

Look at the ALLOWED column — it shows 0. This means no pods can be
evicted, which blocks `kubectl drain` completely.

Also check the deployment replicas:

  kubectl get deploy pdb-app -n ckaquest

You'll see 3/3 replicas ready. The PDB requires ALL 3 to be available.
