Try applying the pod spec below and observe what error you get:

  kubectl apply -f - <<EOF
  apiVersion: v1
  kind: Pod
  metadata:
    name: critical-app
    namespace: ckaquest
  spec:
    priorityClassName: high-priority
    containers:
      - name: app
        image: nginx:1.25
  EOF

You'll see: "no PriorityClass with name high-priority was found"

List existing PriorityClasses (they are cluster-scoped):
  kubectl get priorityclass

You'll see system ones but NOT 'high-priority'. You need to create it first.
