The pod uses dnsPolicy: None with a wrong nameserver IP (10.0.0.1).

When dnsPolicy is None, Kubernetes does NOT inject any DNS settings
automatically. The pod relies entirely on the dnsConfig block you provide.

Find the actual CoreDNS ClusterIP:

  kubectl get svc -n kube-system kube-dns -o jsonpath='{.spec.clusterIP}'

On k3s this is usually 10.43.0.10. You can either:
  (a) Fix the nameserver IP in dnsConfig to match the real CoreDNS IP
  (b) Switch to dnsPolicy: ClusterFirst (the easier option)

Since pods are immutable, you must delete and recreate the dns-client pod.
