Two issues with the multi-svc Service:

1. Multi-port Services REQUIRE named ports. Without names, Kubernetes
   cannot distinguish between them in Ingress rules, NetworkPolicies,
   or endpoint slices.

2. The HTTPS targetPort is 8443, but the container listens on 443.
   Traffic to port 443 on the Service gets forwarded to 8443 on the
   pod — where nothing is listening.

Compare container ports vs service targetPorts:
  kubectl get pod multi-app -n ckaquest -o jsonpath='{.spec.containers[0].ports}'
  kubectl get svc multi-svc -n ckaquest -o jsonpath='{.spec.ports}'
