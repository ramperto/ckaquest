Check why the pod is stuck:

  kubectl describe pod config-app -n ckaquest | grep -A5 "Events:"
  # Warning  FailedMount  ...
  # "MountVolume.SetUp failed for volume \"config-vol\":
  #  configmap \"app-config\" not found"

The volume references a ConfigMap that doesn't exist.
A missing ConfigMap in a volume mount blocks pod startup completely —
the kubelet can't mount the volume so the container never starts.
